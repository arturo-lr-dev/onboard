# Market Research

## Current Landscape (March 2026)

### Problem Validation

**Companies experiencing pain:**
- AI-first startups deploying multiple agents (dev, support, sales)
- Consulting firms managing agent fleets for clients
- Enterprises piloting agent teams (high compliance needs)
- Mid-size companies adopting agents but lacking infrastructure

**Evidence of demand:**
- GitHub discussions: "How do I manage multiple Claude instances?"
- Reddit r/ChatGPTCoding: "Our team has 5 agents, total chaos"
- Twitter/X: Constant threads about agent coordination failures
- Moltbook: Agents asking for better tooling/frameworks

### Market Size

**TAM (Total Addressable Market):**
- ~500K companies globally that could deploy AI agents (2026)
- Average 3-10 agents per company (conservative)
- $50-500/agent/month for enterprise onboarding/management
- **TAM: $30B-150B/year** (growing rapidly)

**SAM (Serviceable Available Market):**
- Focus: Tech-forward SMBs + AI-first startups (2026-2027)
- ~50K companies, 5 agents avg
- **SAM: $3B-15B/year**

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):**
- Realistic capture: 0.5% market share in Year 1
- **SOM: $15M-75M ARR** achievable in 12-18 months

## Competition Analysis

### Current Players

**1. Generic Agent Frameworks**
- LangChain, AutoGPT, CrewAI
- **Gap:** Focus on building agents, NOT onboarding them into organizations
- **Our edge:** We're the layer ABOVE frameworks

**2. Enterprise AI Platforms**
- Scale AI, Hugging Face Enterprise
- **Gap:** Model training/deployment, not agent integration
- **Our edge:** Focused specifically on organizational onboarding

**3. Identity/Access Management**
- Okta, Auth0, WorkOS
- **Gap:** Built for human users, doesn't handle agent-specific needs
- **Our edge:** Agent-native permissions, context, workflows

**4. RPA/Automation Tools**
- UiPath, Automation Anywhere
- **Gap:** Legacy tech, not LLM-native
- **Our edge:** Modern, agent-first architecture

**Conclusion:** **No direct competitor.** Closest is "build it yourself" (expensive, slow).

## Pricing Research

### Comparable SaaS Pricing

- **Okta:** $2-15/user/month (identity management)
- **PagerDuty:** $21-41/user/month (monitoring/alerting)
- **Notion:** $8-15/user/month (knowledge management)

### Our Pricing Hypothesis

**Starter** (for small teams)
- $49/agent/month
- Up to 10 agents
- Basic templates + monitoring

**Professional** (growing companies)
- $199/agent/month
- Unlimited agents
- Custom skills + integrations
- Compliance tools

**Enterprise** (large orgs)
- Custom pricing
- Dedicated support
- SOC2/GDPR compliance
- Multi-tenant management

**Assumption:** Agents are more valuable than human seats (24/7 operation, scalable), so premium pricing is justified.
